About

Research focus

My work expands what circuit quantum electrodynamics (cQED) architectures can do through parametric processes — beam-splitter, cross-Kerr, and two-photon-squeezing interactions — applied to the practical problem of reading out a qubit's state quickly and with near-unity fidelity. Before this, my doctoral research at the Indian Institute of Science centered on cavity electromechanics: using few-unit-cell flakes of a high-Tc superconductor as a mechanical resonator to sense trapped vortex charge.

Current work

Three problems I'm working on

Readout

Time-sequenced parametric qubit readout

A protocol that uses two-photon driving to latch a memory mode onto one of two stable cavity states depending on qubit initialization — achieving near-unity separation fidelity.

Gates

High-fidelity iSWAP gate

Fast two-qubit logic controlled via a double-transmon coupler element, developed in collaboration with the Simmonds and Kamal groups.

Materials

Low-loss dielectrics

Fabricating and characterizing Al/Si/Al parallel-plate capacitors with a crystalline-silicon FIN dielectric to cut energy loss in superconducting circuits.
